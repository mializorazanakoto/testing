ORIGINAL ABSTRACT

Continuous Accumulation of Volatiles in Lunar South Pole Cold Traps: A Multi-Epoch Modeling Approach

Selene Lunin, Orion Vance, et al.

"Recent observations from the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (LRO) suggest that the distribution of water ice in permanently shadowed regions (PSRs) is non-uniform and highly dependent on crater morphology. We present a numerical simulation model that tracks volatile migration and sequestration over a 3.5-billion-year period. Our results indicate that cometary impacts are not the sole source of lunar water; rather, a continuous flux of micro-meteoroids and solar-wind-induced hydroxyl production contributes significantly (approx. 45%) to the total ice volume. This model aligns with current neutron spectrometer data but suggests that subsurface ice layers are much older than previously estimated, with implications for future Artemis landing site selection."

PEER REVIEW REPORT

REVIEWER 2

RECOMMENDATION: MAJOR REVISION / REJECT

While the authors attempt to tackle a relevant problem, the methodology is fundamentally flawed and the conclusions are largely speculative.

1. TEMPORAL FLUX INCONSISTENCY

The model assumes a constant micro-meteoroid flux over 3.5 Ga, which contradicts recent geochemical evidence of the Late Heavy Bombardment tail. The authors must account for decaying flux gradients.

2. LOSS MECHANISM NEGLECT

The claim that solar-wind-induced hydroxyl contributes 45% is unsupported. The authors completely ignore the loss mechanism of UV photolysis during migration phases, likely overestimating retention by 2-3x.

3. SCALE ALIASING ERRORS

The comparison with LRO neutron data is superficial. The resolution of the model (~50km) does not match the 10km scale of the instrument, leading to significant aliasing errors that mask local gradients.

CONCRETE IMPROVEMENTS

  • Update Flux Models: Integrate the *Hartmann 2024* decaying flux curve instead of constant 3.5 Ga averages.
  • Photolytic Depletion: Add a photolysis-rate variable (λp) to the migration equation to account for hydroxyl loss.
  • Nested Meshing: Implement a nested grid (1km resolution) around the Shackleton and Amundsen craters to validate against LRO-LAMP data.
